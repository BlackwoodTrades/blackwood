FAQ
Common questions
What is a prediction market? +
Prediction markets like Kalshi and Polymarket let you trade on the outcome of real-world events — sports results, economic data, elections, and more. You buy contracts that pay out if your predicted outcome is correct. Prices reflect the crowd's implied probability of each outcome happening.
What makes your AI model different? +
Our model calculates its own probability estimate for each contract and compares it to the current market price. When our estimate differs significantly from the crowd's implied odds, that gap is the edge. Most prediction market participants rely on intuition — we use systematic probability modeling.
Do I need accounts on Kalshi and Polymarket? +
Yes — you'll need to create accounts on whichever platforms you want to trade on. Kalshi is US-regulated and accepts USD. Polymarket is crypto-based. Both are free to sign up. The course walks through the setup process for both.
What sports do you cover right now? +
Currently NFL, NBA, and MLB — the highest-liquidity sports markets on both Kalshi and Polymarket. We're expanding to additional sports and non-sports categories as we grow.
What does a signal actually look like? +
Each signal includes: the platform (Kalshi or Polymarket), the specific contract, the current market-implied probability, our model's estimated probability, the edge size (the difference), and our confidence level. Free members see the contract and our predicted outcome. Paid members get the full probability breakdown.
